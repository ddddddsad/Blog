Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff Equation

The Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff (BCH) formula is crucial in quantum mechanics for combining exponentials of non-commuting operators. It is significant in the evolution of operators in the Heisenberg representation.

The formula is expressed as:

\[ e^{\hat{X}}\hat{Y}e^{-\hat{X}} = \hat{Y} + \frac{1}{2}[\hat{X}, \hat{Y}] + \frac{1}{2!}[\hat{X}, [\hat{X}, \hat{Y}]] + \frac{1}{3!}[\hat{X}, [\hat{X}, [\hat{X}, \hat{Y}]]] + \cdots \]

Define \(F(\lambda) = e^{\lambda \hat{A}}\hat{B}e^{-\lambda \hat{A}}\), then

\[ \frac{dF(\lambda)}{d\lambda} = e^{\lambda \hat{A}}\hat{A}\hat{B}e^{-\lambda\hat{A}} - e^{\lambda \hat{A}}\hat{B}\hat{A}e^{-\lambda\hat{A}} = e^{\lambda \hat{A}}[\hat{A}, \hat{B}]e^{-\lambda\hat{A}} = e^{\lambda \hat{A}}C_1e^{-\lambda\hat{A}} \]

\[ \cdots \]

\[ \frac{d^nF(\lambda)}{d\lambda^n} = e^{\lambda \hat{A}}[\hat{A}, C_{n-1}]e^{-\lambda\hat{A}} = e^{\lambda \hat{A}}C_ne^{-\lambda\hat{A}} \]

So that we can define \( C_0 = \hat{B}, C_1 = [\hat{A}, \hat{B}], C_2 = [\hat{A}, C_1]] \cdots, C_n = [\hat{A}, C_{n-1}]] \)

Then we perform a Taylor expansion of \(F(\lambda)\) \[ F(\lambda) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{1}{n!}\lambda^nC_n \] This is the formula above, which is actually just a younger version of the BCH formula.

In the Heisenberg representation, the evolution of operators is given by the rule \( A \rightarrow UAU^{\dagger} \), where \( U \) is the unitary evolution operator. If \( U \) is generated by the Hamiltonian \( H \), then the evolution rule becomes:

\[ A \rightarrow e^{\frac{t}{i\hbar}H} A e^{-\frac{t}{i\hbar}H} \]

This form emphasizes the role of operators in the time evolution of quantum states, preserving the unitary nature and gauge invariance of quantum states. Through this formula, the dynamic behavior of quantum systems at different time points can be studied.

Ex1. Phase Shifter

Hamiltonian is given as \( H = \phi n \), and the annihilation operator \( \hat{a} \) evolves as

\[ e^{-i\phi n}\hat{a}e^{i\phi n} = \hat{a} + i\phi[n, \hat{a}] - \frac{\phi}{2!}[n, [n, \hat{a}]] + \cdots = \hat{a}(1+i\phi-\frac{\phi^2}{2}+\cdots)=e^{i\phi}\hat{a} \]

Ex2. Beam Splitter

Hamiltonian is given as \( H = \theta e^{i\phi}a^{\dagger}b + \theta e^{-i\phi}ab^{\dagger} \), evolving as

\[ e^{-iH}ae^{iH} = cos\theta, a + isin\theta, e^{i\phi}b \]

\[ e^{-iH}be^{iH} = isin\theta, e^{-i\phi}a = cos\theta, b \]

Actually it is Bogliubov Transformation.
